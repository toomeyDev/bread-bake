Easy Whole-Wheat Bagels

Description

These whole-wheat bagels are very simple to make (only 4 ingredients!) and have a rich, hearty texture thanks to the whole-wheat flour.

Very quick to make and bake, this recipe can be mixed and fully baked in under an hour.

For added flavor, add raisins, chopped nuts, cinnamon, sesame seeds or other mixins while stirring the dough.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup whole wheat flour
  • 1 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/3 tsp salt
  • 1 cup plain greek yogurt

[Optional]

  • 1 whisked egg for glazing
  • Toppings (raisins, poppy seeds, cinnamon etc)

Makes 4 servings

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 425F / 218C, line a baking sheet with parchment paper or spray with oil.
  2. In a large mixing bowl, mix flour, baking powder and salt until combined.
  3. Stir in yogurt and mix using a large spoon, stir in any mixins (raisins etc)
  4. Stir until a ball begins to form. Transfer to floured surface and knead approx. 1-2 minutes.
  5. Divide dough into 4 equal pieces, roll into even-sized "logs" of dough.
  6. Shape each log ino a ring, pinch the ends together to form the shape of the bagels.
  7. Place on baking sheet, if adding any toppings, glaze the tops of the bagels with egg and sprinkle desired toppings on the bagels.
  8. Bake for 20 minutes, color should be golden brown.
  9. Place finished bagels on a rack or plate for cooling.

Bagels will keep for around two days in the fridge, or a 1-2 months if frozen immediately.
